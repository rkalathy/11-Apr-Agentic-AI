PERFORMANCE REVIEW POLICY

1. Review Cycle
   - Performance reviews are conducted twice a year: April and October.
   - Mid-year review (October): Progress check and goal realignment.
   - Annual review (April): Comprehensive evaluation with rating and compensation revision.

2. Rating Scale
   - 5 - Exceptional: Consistently exceeds expectations, role model for the team.
   - 4 - Exceeds Expectations: Frequently delivers above what is expected.
   - 3 - Meets Expectations: Consistently meets all job requirements.
   - 2 - Needs Improvement: Falls short in some areas, requires development plan.
   - 1 - Unsatisfactory: Fails to meet minimum expectations, PIP may be initiated.

3. Process
   - Self-assessment: Employee fills out self-review form.
   - Manager review: Manager provides ratings and feedback.
   - Calibration: Department heads calibrate ratings across teams.
   - Final review meeting: Manager discusses feedback with the employee.
   - Goal setting: New goals are set for the next review period.

4. Compensation Revision
   - Salary increments are based on performance rating and market benchmarks.
   - Typical increment ranges:
     - Rating 5: 15-20% increment
     - Rating 4: 10-15% increment
     - Rating 3: 7-10% increment
     - Rating 2: 0-5% increment
     - Rating 1: No increment, Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

5. Promotion Criteria
   - Minimum 2 consecutive ratings of 4 or above.
   - Demonstrated readiness for the next level.
   - Availability of position at the next level.
   - Recommendation from the manager and department head.

6. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
   - Duration: 90 days.
   - Clear, measurable goals are set.
   - Weekly check-ins with the manager.
   - If goals are not met, it may lead to role change or separation.
