Title: Investment Strategies for Senior Investors (Age 55-70) — India Guide 2026

Understanding Risk Appetite by Age:
Senior investors (age 55-70) typically fall in the conservative to moderate risk category. Capital preservation becomes the primary goal, followed by regular income generation. Growth is a secondary objective.

Recommended Asset Allocation for Age 60:
- Fixed Income / Debt: 50-60% (FDs, bonds, debt mutual funds)
- Large-Cap Equity: 20-25% (blue-chip stocks, index funds)
- Dividend-Yield Stocks: 10-15% (companies with consistent payouts)
- Gold / Alternative: 5-10%
- Cash / Liquid: 5%

The "100 Minus Age" Rule:
A common rule of thumb — equity allocation should be (100 - age)%. For a 60-year-old, this means 40% in equity and 60% in fixed income. However, for investors with high monthly income (₹5 lakhs+) and no immediate need for the investment, slightly higher equity allocation (up to 50%) can be considered.

Key Principles for Senior Investors:
1. Prefer dividend-paying stocks over growth stocks
2. Avoid small-cap and micro-cap stocks (high volatility)
3. Focus on companies with at least 10 years of consistent profitability
4. Maintain at least 6 months of expenses in liquid funds
5. Sector concentration should not exceed 30% of equity portfolio
6. Review portfolio quarterly, not daily

Tax Considerations (India, FY 2025-26):
- LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains) on equity: 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh
- STCG (Short Term Capital Gains) on equity: 20%
- Dividend income: Taxed at slab rate
- For ₹50,000 investment, LTCG exemption covers most gains in the first year

Suitable Investment Vehicles for ₹50,000:
1. Direct equity in 2-3 large-cap food companies: ₹25,000-30,000
2. Food/FMCG sector mutual fund (SIP or lump sum): ₹10,000-15,000
3. Keep ₹5,000-10,000 as buffer for averaging down

Expected Returns (Based on Historical Data):
- Conservative (large-cap, dividend stocks): 10-12% annually
- Moderate (mix of large and mid-cap): 14-16% annually
- Optimistic (if sector tailwinds continue): 18-22% annually

Warning: Past performance does not guarantee future results. Equity investments carry market risk. Senior investors should consult a SEBI-registered financial advisor before making investment decisions.
